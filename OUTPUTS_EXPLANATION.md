## 📊 Detailed Explanation of SHAP Visualizations (`outputs/shap/`)

### 1. SHAP Summary Plot (`shap_summary.png`)
* **What it is:** A global summary combining feature importance and feature direction.
* **How to read it:** 
  - Features are sorted on the y-axis by their overall importance (most important at the top).
  - The x-axis shows the SHAP value, which represents the impact on the model's output (Compressive Strength in MPa).
  - Points represents individual samples.
  - The color of each point represents the feature value: **Red** indicates high values, and **Blue** indicates low values.
* **Engineering Interpretation:**
  - **Silica Fume Content** is the top feature. Red points are clustered on the positive side (right of 0), meaning higher silica fume content directly drives higher compressive strength.
  - **Water Content** shows red points clustered on the negative side (left of 0), meaning that higher water content degrades compressive strength.

### 2. SHAP Beeswarm Plot (`shap_beeswarm.png`)
* **What it is:** A detailed distribution plot showing the density and spread of impact for each feature.
* **How to read it:**
  - Similar to the summary plot, but points are stacked vertically to show the density distribution of individual sample attributions.
  - High density areas appear thicker (like a vertical bubble).
* **Engineering Interpretation:**
  - Shows that curing temperature and fiber reinforcements have tight, high-density clusters on the positive side, indicating stable, predictable positive contributions.
  - The wide spread of silica fume indicates that compressive strength is highly sensitive to its variation.

### 3. SHAP Global Bar Plot (`shap_bar.png`)
* **What it is:** Global feature importance ranking.
* **How to read it:**
  - Displays the mean absolute SHAP value: $\text{mean}(|\text{SHAP value}|)$ for each feature.
  - Ranks features by their average magnitude of impact on the compressive strength prediction (in MPa).
* **Engineering Interpretation:**
  - Quantifies feature impact: e.g., changing Silica Fume content shifts the compressive strength prediction by an average of $\approx 10.5 \text{ MPa}$ globally across all mixtures.

### 4. SHAP Waterfall Plot (`shap_waterfall.png`)
* **What it is:** A local, mix-specific attribution breakdown for a single sample (defaults to Sample #0).
* **How to read it:**
  - **Base Value ($E[f(X)]$):** The average compressive strength predicted by the model across the entire dataset (displayed at the bottom, e.g., $\approx 92 \text{ MPa}$).
  - **Attributions (Arrows):** Red values ($+$) show features that increased the strength for this specific mix, while Blue values ($-$) show features that reduced it.
  - **Prediction ($f(x)$):** The final predicted compressive strength for this mix design (displayed at the top).
* **Engineering Interpretation:**
  - Explains exactly why a specific mix achieved a high strength (e.g., $118 \text{ MPa}$) by showing the step-by-step contributions: e.g., $+15.2 \text{ MPa}$ due to silica fume, $+8.4 \text{ MPa}$ due to 60°C curing, and $-3.1 \text{ MPa}$ due to water.

### 5. Interactive SHAP Force Plot (`shap_force.html`)
* **What it is:** An interactive HTML component showing the "forces" balancing a single prediction.
* **How to read it:**
  - Open this file in any web browser.
  - Features pushing the prediction higher are represented by red blocks on the left, pushing to the right.
  - Features pushing the prediction lower are represented by blue blocks on the right, pushing to the left.
  - The interface allows hovering over blocks to see original material value scales.

### 6. SHAP Dependence Plots (`dependence_feature_1..5.png`)
* **What it is:** Single-feature scatter plots showing the relationship between a feature's values and its SHAP values, with automatic interaction coloring.
* **How to read it:**
  - **X-axis:** The original unscaled values of the feature (e.g., Silica Fume content in kg/m³).
  - **Y-axis:** The SHAP value (impact on strength in MPa).
  - **Coloring:** Points are colored by the values of the feature that has the strongest statistical interaction.
* **Interpretation of Top 5 Dependence Plots:**
  1. **Silica Fume Content (`dependence_feature_1.png`):** Shows a non-linear upward curve. The SHAP value is negative below 35 kg/m³ and becomes highly positive up to 80 kg/m³, showing the pozzolanic activation threshold.
  2. **PP Fiber Content (`dependence_feature_2.png`):** Rises quickly up to 5 kg/m³ ($+12 \text{ MPa}$ impact) and plateaus, demonstrating that adding fibers beyond a certain threshold yields diminishing returns.
  3. **PP Fiber volume fraction (`dependence_feature_3.png`):** Shows a strong interaction with Curing Temperature. At high curing temperatures (red), the fiber bridging effect is enhanced, leading to higher SHAP values.
  4. **Water Content (`dependence_feature_4.png`):** Shows a linear downward slope. Below 190 kg/m³, SHAP impact is positive ($+5 \text{ MPa}$). Above 210 kg/m³, the impact drops sharply into negative values ($-15 \text{ MPa}$).
  5. **Superplasticizer Content (`dependence_feature_5.png`):** Rises up to 12 kg/m³ and plateaus. This shows the optimum dosage of superplasticizer needed to maintain workability while minimizing water content.
